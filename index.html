Make connections without infection

Treatment lets U be U without spreading HIV

Having HIV doesn't have to change your lifestyle. In fact, people who take HIV treatment as prescribed can maintain an undetectable viral load. And undetectable = untransmittable. Now you can be who you want with whom you want, and there's zero risk of spreading HIV.

Don't be risky when you get frisky

You can be a yeti in the streets and a dragon in the sheets. However you play it, play it safe. Use condoms, PrEP (a once-daily pill for HIV prevention) or PEP (morning-after treatment for emergency HIV prevention). Choose the protection that fits your lifestyle so you can be sex positive while staying HIV negative.

Just a little spit and that's it

HIV testing is easier than ever with free home test kits

Knowing your HIV status is never a bad thing. No, really. If you're HIV positive, you can start the treatment you need to achieve an undetectable viral load (U=U). And if you're HIV negative, you can keep it that way by protecting yourself going forward. So stop making excuses and start getting tested.

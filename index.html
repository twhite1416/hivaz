Make connections without infection

Treatment lets U be U without spreading HIV

Having HIV doesn't have to change your lifestyle. In fact, people who take HIV medications as prescribed can maintain an undetectable viral load. And undetectable = untransmittable. Now you can be who you want with whom you want, and there's zero risk of spreading HIV.

Don't be risky when you get frisky

You can be a yeti in the streets and a dragon in the sheets. However you play it, play it safe. Use condoms, PrEP (a once-daily pill for HIV prevention) or PEP (morning-after treatment for emergency HIV prevention). Choose the protection that fits your lifestyle so you can be sex positive while staying HIV negative.

PrEP

What’s PrEP? It’s Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP). It’s a pill you take once a day to help prevent HIV infection. If you are HIV negative and want to go on PrEP, you’ll have to get tested to confirm your status along with other lab tests. You must also commit to taking the medication every day and to visit your medical provider every 3 months for follow-up HIV tests and prescription refills.

Just a little spit and that's it

HIV testing is easier than ever with free home test kits

Knowing your HIV status is a good thing. No, really. If you're HIV positive, you can start the treatment you need to achieve an undetectable viral load (U=U) and stay healthy. And if you're HIV negative, you can keep it that way by protecting yourself going forward. So stop making excuses and start getting tested.

Positive or negative, insured or not - you've got options.

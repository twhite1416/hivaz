Treatment lets U be U without spreading HIV

Having HIV doesn't have to change your lifestyle. In fact, people who take HIV medications as prescribed can maintain an undetectable viral load. And undetectable = untransmittable. Now you can be who you want with whom you want, and there's zero risk of spreading HIV.

Choose the protection that fits your lifestyle so you can be sex positive while staying HIV negative.

PrEP

Staying HIV negative is easier than ever thanks to Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP). By taking this pill once a day, you can help prevent HIV infection. To get started, simply visit your medical providers for lab tests and an HIV test to confirm your negative status. To be effective, you must take PrEP daily and follow up with your medical provider every 3 months.

PEP

Condoms and PrEP should always be your first choices in preventing HIV infection. But if an accident should happen, PEP is there to help. PEP stands for Post-Exposure Prophylaxis and can be used for morning-after prevention. To be effective, PEP treatment must begin within 72 hours of exposure and continue for 28 days. Your specific PEP treatment will be determined by your medical provider based on how you were exposed to HIV.
